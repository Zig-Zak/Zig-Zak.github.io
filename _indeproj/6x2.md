## Overview
Project 6x2 is a **dual-front-wheel steering** 1:28 scale micro RC platform inspired by modern pan-car type RC. It is designed for swift and precise locomotion over mildly dusty or low-friction flat surfaces, such as tiles or wooden floors in public areas. This Project is open-sourced on [Printables.com](https://www.printables.com/model/1505785-6x2-quad-front-steering-car-128-rc-car), with detailed building tutorial and parts catalog.

>- Minimum Turning Radius: ~20cm
>- Drive Train: RWD, 130 brushed motor(23,600RPM, ~1.5-2.0mNm @max 3V,9w)
>- Traction Config.: 2x Dia20mm W14mm rear tires, 4x Dia20mm W8.5mm front tires
>- Power Source: 7.4v LiPo Battery
>- Top Speed: 27km/h(30A esc, 7.2:1 gear ratio), 19km/h(10a esc, 7.2:1 gear ratio)
>- Other Systems: adjustable ESP(single axis gyroscope, no TCS)

## Conceptual Design

### Inspiration: The Pan Car Chassis
>The design of the 6x2 draws heavily from pan-car–style RC cars. Pan car referes to a type of RC car dedicated to Carpet/Asphalt on-road racing with a unique chassis design. Unlike a full-scale vehicle, a typical pan car uses solid rear axle suspension where motors and rear wheels are mounted together. Such design maximizes drivetrain efficiency but sacrifices on unsprung mass.

>The rear suspension assembly usually has two degrees of freedom: vertical travel and twist along the main axis, each supported by dedicated springs and dampers, so that each of these Dofs can be adjusted independently.

---

Typical Pan cars often intend to place their batteries as rearward as possible and use oversized rear wheels, in order to maximize static load on the rear axle. This setup <i>**promoted the understeering aspect**</i> of vehicles, which may feel counterintuitive, since in full-scale racing, oversteer-prone setup rather yields faster lap times. 

In fact, on pan cars, <i>**oversteering setups are ineffective**</i> due to two major factors:
  1. Foam tires
   >Most Pan cars employ foam tires that require lower operating temperature and is more prone to mechanical wearing than rubber. Oversteering tendency increases temperature and wear outs, which goes against the characteristic of foam tires. 
  2. Rapid oversteering dynamics
  >In the world of RCs, oversteering develops on a time scale too short to be practically controlled, and this effect becomes increasingly severe as vehicle scale decreases. As a result, factory setups usually lean towards understeering.

>The 6×2 adopts most of the conventional pan car features, but keeps side links as the sole connection between the motor mount and the main chassis. These side links are also longer than those of a typical pan car while featuring adjustable rear suspension droop (downward travel).

>These design choices **improve the smoothness of the rear suspension motion**, which in turn increases the limit of forward traction under acceleration. The extended droop also **lowers the rear roll center**, smoothing lateral load transfer and raising the control limit during rapid left-right transitions. On paper, this might sounds like a pure win, and in practice, it is.  With no need to fit standard off-the-shelf bodies (there simply aren’t any for six-wheel cars), the 6×2 benefits from little constraints over chassis layout, making room for a longer-travel, larger-scale rear suspension system than other RC cars of its size.

>Albeit the similarities, the 6×2 is not just a replication of a six-wheel pan car. By purposefully **allocating primary mass components, namely the motor, battery, and servo, along the centerline**, the 6×2 reinforces its most critical advantage: the **straight-line stability provided by its long wheelbase and abundant grip contacts**. Masses along the centerline increases the vehicle’s moment of inertia about the steering axis, which, together with its flexible suspension design, further elevates the limit of forward traction. 

>The **Ackeman Geometry** was also taken into account. In theory, the front steering wheels should turn more than the mid wheels due to their different distance to the instantaneous center of rotation. The calculation is shown in the image below. The mathematical result suggested that front pair should steer at an average of ~1.14x rate of the mid. However, in practice, as smaller RC car has higher traction/weight ratio, the car was not load-sensitive enough to have ackerman geometry playing a noticable role in handling.

As a result, the 6x2 can withstand immensely strong forward acceleration. In testing with a 30A ESC, the 6x2 can launch at full throttle without a gyro and maintain an almost perfectly straight line. Most impressively, this was achieved in a dusty tile hallway. Its straight-line stability far exceeds that of similarly sized open-class racers. 

## Limitations

However, this design also brings a drawback: The relatively narrow front track width make the 6x2 prone to traction rolling on high-grip surfaces, a special behavior mostly seen on smaller RC cars, when the grip is so strong that instead of sliding, the car tends to just tip over on a sharp turn. 

>This "narrow wheel track" design was intended to reduce the influence of imperfect Ackerman Geometry. However, as previously mentioned, this feature wasn't practically significant. Thus, in the future, a wider chassis with longer front suspension travel will be developed to compensate for this issue.

For now, 6x2 is yet on pair with open class racers on high grip RCP(foam tile) tracks. The on track testings at [Brooklyn Hobbies](https://www.google.com/maps?client=firefox-b-1-d&hs=NWKU&sca_esv=cac5c59478b937dc&output=search&q=brooklyn+hobbies&source=lnms&fbs=AIIjpHxU7SXXniUZfeShr2fp4giZjSkgYzz5-5RrRWAIniWd7tzPwkE1KJWcRvaH01D-XIUAvg3n_tnU4u8lZtcCOD0ElNEgYLABq5AJNtEpcRNeWCN8Edzb7TNFl45cVKWapq11NYwWTMMMspLQm7tcrbaxP7_5qYBm8N_xcJki8mH9KBmUSS-InYWVDCWS2a7XOZ5_rKgs9P0dcYJPXJ0h_N4714UuXw&entry=mc&ved=1t:200715&ictx=111) indicated that the 6x2, equipped with a 10A ESC, was:
- 53% faster than a Mini-Z RWD with similar power(6.5 s/lap vs. 10 s/lap)
- 23% faster than modified Mini-Z RWD cars with ~1.5× the power(6.5 s/lap vs. 8 s/lap)
- Slower than local top-tier open-class cars (4.5 s/lap), while those cars theoretically draw 8 – 12 × more power.

>The power ratings are estimated from online product catalogs of the ESCs and discussions from hobbyist forums.

## Other Results

On the other hand, the excessive grip of 6x2 is **advantageous on low-friction surfaces** such as household wooden or tile floors. 
>The quad-front steering layout turns the front of the car a capable load-bearing platform, suitable for mounting devices such as action cameras or FPV systems. This forward mounting position reduces visual obstruction and provides a clearer field of view, especially when compared with conventional four-wheel layouts or truck-style layout.
